## Summarizing documents and media using Alexa
**Short Description:** You can use Alexa to summarize long text files, complex articles, or emails. You can also ask Alexa to convert these summaries into a conversational, audio podcast format.

### Prerequisites

Before you begin, make sure you have the following:
- An active Amazon account that has access to Alexa plus features.
- The Amazon Alexa app installed on your mobile device, or access to the web portal.
To summarize documents or generate media briefings.

Steps to follow to summarize your documents or generate custom media briefings:

1 Open the Amazon Alexa app or navigate to the web portal.

2 You can upload, forward, or drag-and-drop your target source file, such as a PDF, school email, or a photo of a schedule, into the Alexa interface.
 Note: The maximum file size for document uploads is 25 MB. Supported formats include PDF, TXT, DOCX, and JPEG.
 
3 Wake your device and state your request to analyze the document.
- For a text overview, say: 'Alexa, summarize the document I just shared with you."
- To automatically extract dates and schedule notes, say: "Alexa, add the events from this document to my calendar."

4 To convert your uploaded files or an online web topic into an interactive audio summary, request a media briefing.
 - For an uploaded document, say: "Alexa, turn my rert into a podcast."
 - For a general web topic, say: "Alexa, create a podcast about Atomic Habits."

5 While listening to the generated audio briefing, use voice commands to customize the playback settings.
- To change the length, say: "Alexa, make the summary shorter."
- To change the delivery style, say: "Alexa, change your personality style to Chill."
  
6 Confirm completion of the task by listening for Alexa's confirmation responses:
- Confirmation message: "I have processed your document. Here is a summary of the main points."
- Confirmation message: "Your custom podcast is ready. Let's dive into the topic.”
